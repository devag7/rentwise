## Positioning

**RentWise doesn't sell listings. It sells verdicts.**

Every other platform shows you inventory and lets you guess. RentWise cross-references scraped multi-source market data to tell you what a flat is actually worth — before you waste a weekend or a deposit. Zero brokers, zero forced logins, zero invented data.

One-sentence positioning: *The price-truth layer for Bangalore rentals.*

### Audience

- **Primary segment**: tech professionals, 24–35, relocating to or upgrading within Bangalore. Time-poor, data-literate, broker-averse.
  - **Anchor persona**: Priya, 27, SDE-2, moving from Pune for a Koramangala job. Two weekends to find a 1BHK. Has been burned by fake photos twice. Trusts dashboards more than salespeople.
- **Secondary segments**: (1) young couples consolidating into a 2BHK, (2) small landlords with 1–3 units who want direct tenants without paying brokers.

## Mission, values, story

**Mission**: make every Bangalore rent price honest.

**Values**
1. **Verdicts over vibes** — every claim carries a number.
2. **Zero fake data** — scraped fields are never invented; gaps stay visible.
3. **The renter is the customer** — no broker money, no ranking games.
4. **Speed is respect** — answers in seconds, not site visits.

**Story** (brand voice)
Bangalore rents are a rumor market. The same 1BHK is ₹22,000 on one portal, ₹31,000 on another, and "just gone, but I have something similar" on the phone. RentWise was built to end the rumor: we pull every listing we can find, strip the marketing, and price it against the actual market. What's left is a number you can negotiate with. Stop guessing. Know what it's worth.

## Voice & tone

**Adjectives**: declarative, numeric, dry-witted, unhurried, anti-fluff.

**Rules**
- Lead with the number. "₹4,200 over market" beats "great value!"
- Max three sentences per block. If it needs four, it needs a table.
- Verdict language: *over market / at market / under market.* Never "steal", "dream home", "luxurious".
- UPPERCASE mono microtype for labels, sentence case for everything else.

**Copy examples**
- Headline: `This 2BHK is ₹6,000 over market.`
- Subhead: `Based on 1,432 comparable listings in HSR Layout, updated 06:00 IST.`
- Button: `SHOW THE COMPS`
- Error: `Market data is syncing. Verdicts return in about a minute.`
- Empty state: `Zero matches. Loosen one filter — rent ceiling moves results most.`
- Social caption: `Indiranagar 1BHKs are up 9% this quarter. Your landlord knows. Now you do.`

**Forbidden**: premium, luxury, dream home, cozy vibes, hassle-free, curated, elevate, "perfect for any occasion", exclamation marks in product UI.

**Pairings**: Signal on Ink, Paper on Ink, Ink on Paper, Verified on Ink. Never Signal on Verified or vice versa. Never Signal text below 18px on Ink (contrast).

## Typography

- **Display — Bricolage Grotesque** (wght 800, tracking -0.03em). Headlines, wordmark, big numbers. https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Bricolage+Grotesque
- **Data/labels — Reddit Mono** (wght 600, tracking +0.12em, uppercase). Stats, labels, table figures, microtype. https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Reddit+Mono
- **Body — Instrument Sans** (wght 400/500). Paragraphs, UI copy. https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Instrument+Sans

**Hierarchy**: H1 64px/1.05 display · H2 40px display · H3 24px display · body 17px/1.6 · caption 12px mono uppercase · min size 11px.

## Logo

- **Wordmark**: `RENTWISE.` in Bricolage Grotesque 800, tight tracking, with the full stop set in Signal coral. The coral period is the ownable detail — *the verdict, full stop.* Never omit it; never recolor it except all-Ink / all-Paper monochrome variants.
- **Symbol**: the **Fair-Value Gauge** — a rounded square frame holding a horizontal midline (the market line) and a coral dot sitting above it (a price under judgment). Reads at 16×16 (favicon) and 512×512 (app icon). Vector-authored SVG; master files in `logo/`.
- **Lockup**: symbol left, wordmark right, gap = symbol width × 0.4. Clear space = height of the wordmark "R" on all sides.
- **Don'ts**: no stretch, no rotation, no drop shadow, no gradients, no recoloring the period outside mono variants, never set the wordmark in another font.

## Imagery

Photography-led. Rules:
- Found, not staged: real Bangalore interiors, window light, slight clutter, film grain.
- People: racially and bodily diverse, never looking at camera, mid-task.
- Color cast: neutral with warm shadows; no HDR, no studio strobes, no stock smiles.
- Data overlays (verdict chips, price lines) may sit on photos — body copy may not.
- Forbidden: glass-tower skylines at dusk, handshake closeups, keys-in-palm clichés, AI-smooth surfaces.

## Do & Don't

**Do**
1. Put the verdict number in the first five words.
2. Use full-bleed Ink sections with one coral element max per screen.
3. Set every stat in Reddit Mono — numbers are the brand.
4. Show the comp count behind any claim ("based on 1,432 listings").
5. End campaign lines with the coral period motif.

**Don't**
1. Don't use Signal and Verified in the same lockup or chart series.
2. Don't write "premium/luxury/dream" — price truth is the premium.
3. Don't crop the wordmark's period — it's the logo's signature.
4. Don't stage photography; found beats staged every time.
5. Don't add a second display font, ever.
